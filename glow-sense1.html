KKM vs NPRA: Know the Difference

Just because a product has the KKM logo doesn't mean it's safe.

You need to know who actually checks and approves your cosmetics.

⚠️ A KKM logo can be printed by anyone. Only NPRA provides official NOT numbers that prove a product is approved and safe to use!

KKM

  • Ministry of Health Malaysia.
  • The main health authority in Malaysia.
  • Does not inspect or approve cosmetic products.
  • Does not issue NOT numbers.
  • Logo use is not controlled for cosmetics.

NPRA

  • National Pharmaceutical Regulatory Agency.
  • A specialized agency under KKM.
  • Issues NOT numbers for cosmetics.
  • Handles cosmetic notifications and medicine.
  • Only NPRA-approved products are truly verified.

How to Spot Safe Beauty Products Like a Pro!

📲 Check it Online!
Hop on npra.gov.my and type in the NOT number to make sure it’s legit.

🚫 No NOT number? RUN!
If you don’t see it, the product might be risky — better safe than sorry!

UNSAFE Product Clues ⚠️

• No ingredient list ⚠️
• Packaging that looks suspicious⚠️
• Intense chemical smell ⚠️
• Colors that look too bright, neon ⚠️
• Immediate skin reactions like stinging, or rashes ⚠️

‘Organic’ Isn't Always Safe

“Organic” doesn’t mean it’s been tested or registered.
Don’t be fooled by labels !

Always check if it’s NPRA-approved, even if it says “natural”.
